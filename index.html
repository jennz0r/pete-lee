Whatever it takes to tell it stunningly.

Pete Lee is a San Francisco-based director, who grew up during the golden age of kung-fu cinema and immigrated from subtropical Taiwan to New England as a teen. Missing the hell out of the glorious kung-fu mayhem, Lee tried to recreate stage action scenes of his own. Eventually Lee moved to San Francisco, where he ran an art program for homeless youths by day and filmed on weekends.

Lee then started applying the wit, grit, and kinetics of kung-fu movies to other stories. He collaborated with housing activists, animators, musicians, comedians, and a famous dwarf cat, growing an unlikely community.

Currently, Lee is preparing his short film ”Don't Be a Hero” for a festival run. His latest documentary project, “Johnny Physical Lives” premiered at HotDocs 2016. In his spare time, Lee sponsors a kung-fu movie series and runs pop-up fundraisers for those affected by the Bay Area housing crisis. He does not know how to ride a bike.
